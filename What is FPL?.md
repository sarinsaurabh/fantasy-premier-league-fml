
# What is Fantasy Premier League (FPL)?

## Introduction to the Premier League

The **Premier League** is the top tier of English professional football (soccer). It consists of 20 teams competing in a 38-match season, running from August to May each year. These teams battle for the title, European qualification spots, and to avoid relegation. With global viewership in the hundreds of millions, it's one of the most followed football leagues in the world.

## What is Fantasy Premier League?

Fantasy Premier League is an online fantasy football game where players create virtual teams composed of real Premier League players and earn points based on those players' actual on-pitch performance. Instead of managing a club, you're a virtual "manager" building a squad within strict budget and squad constraints.

Every gameweek (typically a week during the season), points are awarded based on:
- **Goals scored** (+5 for outfield players, +4 for goalkeepers)
- **Assists** (+3 points)
- **Clean sheets** (+4 for defenders/goalkeepers)
- **Bonus points** for man-of-the-match performances
- **Penalties saved** (+5 for goalkeepers)
- **Minutes played** (1 point per 90 minutes)
- **Yellow/red cards** (-1/-3 points)

Your total score is the sum of all gameweeks' points, and you compete against friends, regional leagues, or the global community.

The player base is diverse, but united by the love of football and strategic thinking.

## Timeline & Season Structure

### The FPL Season
- **August - May**: Main Premier League season with 38 gameweeks
- **Summer**: Pre-season planning and player research
- **Transfer Window**: Two main windows (summer and January) where real Premier League clubs buy/sell players

### Gameweek Structure
Each gameweek typically involves:
- **Deadline**: Transfers and captain/armband choices close (usually Friday 11:00 AM GMT)
- **Fixtures**: Matches are played across Thursday/Friday/Saturday/Sunday
- **Points Awarded**: Scores are updated live as matches conclude
- **Rankings Updated**: Final standings released by Monday

### Key Dates
- **Gameweek 1-19**: First half of the season (autumn/winter)
- **Winter Break**: Typically late December through early January
- **Gameweek 20-38**: Second half of the season (spring/summer)
- **Deadline Day**: January transfer window deadline affects team availability

## FPL Rules & Key Mechanics

### Squad Structure
- **Squad Size**: 15 players total
- **Budget**: Â£100 million to spend on your squad
- **Position Limits**:
  - 2 Goalkeepers (GK)
  - 5 Defenders (DEF)
  - 5 Midfielders (MID)
  - 3 Forwards (FWD)

### Playing 11
- You must select exactly 11 players each gameweek
- Typically: 1 GK, 3-5 DEF, 3-5 MID, 1-2 FWD
- 4 substitutes on the bench

### Transfers
- Each gameweek you get 1 free transfer
- Additional transfers incur a -4 point penalty each
- Unused transfers don't carry over (except when using the Free Hit chip)

### Captain & Vice-Captain
- Your captain gets **double points** for that gameweek
- Vice-captain gets normal points if captain doesn't play
- Strategic captaincy can swing your rank dramatically

## FPL Chips: Game-Changing Tools

Chips are special abilities that give you temporary advantages. You get one use of each chip per season:

### **Wildcard** (2 uses)
- Make unlimited transfers in one gameweek with no points penalty
- 1 free use in the first half (Gameweeks 1-19)
- 1 free use in the second half (Gameweeks 20-38)
- Typically used for major squad overhauls after fixture analysis or injury crisis

### **Free Hit**
- Make unlimited transfers in one gameweek that are **reverted** after that gameweek
- Your original squad returns for the next gameweek
- Useful for playing short-term fixtures favorably without permanent squad changes
- Used for targeting specific high-scoring gameweeks

### **Bench Boost**
- All players on your bench score points as if they played in that gameweek
- Useful when you have quality bench players and an unfavorable starting XI for that week
- Strategic timing is key

### **Triple Captain**
- Your captain scores **3x points** instead of 2x for that gameweek
- High-risk, high-reward chip
- Often used when a star player has exceptional fixtures

## Strategy & Meta

- **Fixture Difficulty**: Planning transfers around favorable upcoming fixtures
- **Form Over Fixtures**: In-form budget players often outscore expensive out-of-form players
- **Differentials**: Choosing players others don't to gain rank advantage
- **Essential Assets**: Top scorers you almost have to own regardless of cost
- **Bench Strategy**: Having valuable bench options for emergencies
- **Hit Timing**: Taking -4 point hits for strategic transfers in high-upside gameweeks
